# Diversification

## The idea
Diversification means spreading investments across many holdings so that no single one can badly damage the whole portfolio. When holdings don't all move together, the ups and downs partly cancel out, reducing overall volatility without necessarily reducing expected long-term return. It is often called the only "free lunch" in investing.

## Levels of diversification
- **Across stocks** — holding many companies rather than one or two.
- **Across sectors** — e.g. not being entirely in banks or entirely in IT, since sectors move differently.
- **Across asset classes** — mixing equity, debt/bonds, and gold, which tend to behave differently in the same conditions.
- **Across geographies** — domestic and international exposure.

## How index funds help
A single Nifty 100 index fund already spreads money across 100 large companies and many sectors, so it provides broad stock-level and sector-level diversification in one holding. It does **not**, by itself, diversify across asset classes — it is entirely equity, so it still carries full equity-market risk.

## The limit of diversification
Diversification reduces company- and sector-specific risk, but it **cannot remove market risk** — if the whole market falls, a diversified equity portfolio falls too. That is why asset allocation (mixing equity with debt and other assets) matters alongside diversification within equities.
