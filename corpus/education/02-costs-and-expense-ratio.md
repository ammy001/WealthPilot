# Costs and the Expense Ratio

## What the expense ratio (TER) is
The **Total Expense Ratio (TER)** is the annual fee a fund charges, expressed as a percentage of the money you have invested. If a fund's TER is 0.20%, you pay ₹200 a year for every ₹1,00,000 invested. It is deducted automatically from the fund's NAV, so you never see a separate bill — but it reduces your return every year.

## Why "low-cost" matters
Because the fee is charged every year and compounds, small differences add up over long periods. Two funds tracking the **same** index will deliver almost the same gross return, so the one with the lower TER tends to leave the investor with more. This is why index funds — which typically have low expense ratios — are often described as "low-cost."

## What counts as low-cost in India
Index funds and ETFs in India commonly have expense ratios that are a small fraction of a percent, well below typical actively managed equity funds. Direct plans cost less than regular plans because they exclude distributor commission.

## Other costs to be aware of
- **Exit load** — a fee some funds charge if you redeem within a set period.
- **Brokerage / demat charges** — apply to ETFs bought on an exchange.
- **Taxes** — gains are taxed separately from fund fees (short-term vs long-term capital gains).

When comparing two similar funds, the expense ratio is one of the clearest, most reliable differences to look at.
