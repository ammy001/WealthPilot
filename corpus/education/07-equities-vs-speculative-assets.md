# Equities vs Speculative Assets (incl. Crypto)

## Why the distinction matters
Not all "investments" carry the same kind of risk. Diversified equities represent ownership of real businesses that earn profits over time. **Speculative assets** — such as cryptocurrencies — have no earnings or cash flows behind them; their price depends almost entirely on what the next buyer will pay. This makes their swings far larger and harder to reason about.

## Volatility comparison
- A broad equity index (e.g. Nifty 100) can fall meaningfully in a bad year but is diversified across 100 established companies.
- Cryptocurrencies have historically shown **much larger** drawdowns — falls of 50–80% from peaks have occurred more than once — and can move sharply in either direction over short periods.

## The danger of "all-in" moves
Concentrating a whole portfolio into a single volatile asset removes the protection that diversification and asset allocation provide. A large, undiversified bet can produce outsized gains **or** severe, lasting losses. This is the opposite of the risk-managed approach behind index investing and asset allocation.

## An educational way to think about speculative assets
General financial education commonly suggests that if someone chooses to hold a highly speculative asset at all, they keep it to a **small, capped portion** of the portfolio — money they could afford to lose entirely — rather than a core holding. How much, if any, is appropriate depends on the individual's risk tolerance and goals.

This material explains the trade-offs; it does not recommend buying, selling, or holding any specific asset.
