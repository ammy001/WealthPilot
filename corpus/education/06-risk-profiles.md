# Risk Profiles (Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive)

Investors are often grouped into broad **risk profiles** that summarise how much volatility and potential loss they are comfortable with. These are **illustrative frameworks for education**, not prescriptions — a real profile depends on an individual's goals, time horizon, income stability, and temperament.

## Conservative
- **Priority:** protecting capital; steady, modest growth.
- **Comfort with swings:** low — large short-term falls are hard to tolerate.
- **Typical horizon:** shorter, or income needed soon.
- **Illustrative allocation:** roughly 20–40% equity, 55–70% debt, 5–10% gold/cash.

## Moderate (balanced)
- **Priority:** balance growth and stability.
- **Comfort with swings:** medium — can accept moderate falls for better long-term growth.
- **Typical horizon:** medium to long.
- **Illustrative allocation:** roughly 50–60% equity, 30–40% debt, 5–10% gold.

## Aggressive
- **Priority:** maximise long-term growth.
- **Comfort with swings:** high — willing to ride out large drawdowns.
- **Typical horizon:** long (many years, no near-term need for the money).
- **Illustrative allocation:** roughly 75–90% equity, 5–20% debt, small satellite/alternatives.

## How this connects to product choice
- A **broad index fund** (all equity) sits at the higher-risk end and is often used as the equity core within any profile — the *amount* allocated to it is what changes across profiles.
- Highly volatile or speculative assets are generally kept to a **small, capped share**, if used at all, especially for conservative investors.

The percentages above are ranges commonly cited in general financial education; they are starting points for discussion, not targets to act on.
